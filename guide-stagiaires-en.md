## ðŸŽ¨ Customizing Your Accordion

One of the great pleasures of this workshop is creating a **unique and personalized** instrument according to your tastes. Here are all available options:

### 1. Wood Species

The choice of wood affects the aesthetics, weight and slightly the resonance of the instrument.

#### ðŸ’ **Cherry Wood (Merisier)**

![Cherry wood sample showing reddish-brown tone](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/cherrywood.png)

- **Appearance**: Warm reddish-brown color, darkens slightly over time
- **Characteristics**: Medium-hard wood, fine and homogeneous grain
- **Popular for**: Its elegant and classic appearance

#### ðŸŒ° **Walnut (Noyer)**

![Walnut wood sample showing rich dark brown color](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/walnut.png)

- **Appearance**: Rich dark brown with pronounced grain patterns
- **Characteristics**: Dense wood, very resistant
- **Popular for**: Its noble character and grain patterns

#### ðŸ **Maple Sycamore (Ã‰rable sycomore)**

![Maple sycamore wood sample showing light creamy color with fine grain](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/maple.png)

- **Appearance**: Light cream to white color, very fine grain
- **Characteristics**: Hard wood, excellent for resonance
- **Popular for**: Its bright and modern appearance

### 2. Right Hand Configuration (melody keyboard)

#### Number of Buttons
- **21 buttons (2 rows)**: Simpler configuration, ideal for beginners
- **33 buttons (3 rows)**: More melodic possibilities, allows playing in multiple keys

#### Layouts (note arrangement)
- **Heim layout**: Traditional German arrangement
- **Milleret-Pignol layout**: French arrangement, very widespread
- **Custom layout***: On request, with additional cost

#### Available Keys
- **G/C** (Sol/Do): Most common, perfect for French and Irish traditional music
- **D/G** (RÃ©/Sol): Higher key, appreciated for Irish music
- **A/D** (La/RÃ©): Very high key, used for certain specific repertoires

#### Tuning Type
- **Dry tuning**: Clear and precise sound, well-defined notes
- **Semi-dry tuning**: Slight wavering, warmer "French" sound

#### Button Marking
- **Tactile marking**: Raised buttons on certain reference notes
- **Visual marking**: Buttons of different colors
- **No marking**: All identical buttons (for advanced musicians)

### 3. Left Hand Configuration (basses and chords)

#### Number of Basses
- **8 basses**: Simple configuration, perfect for beginners
- **12 basses**: More accompaniment possibilities
- **18 basses**: Maximum harmonic richness

#### Layouts
- **Milleret-Pignol layout**: French standard
- **Heim layout**: German standard
- **Custom layout***: Possibility to create your own arrangement (with surcharge)

### 4. Buttons and Mechanics

Three options for keyboard buttons:

- **Natural wood**: Authentic and traditional appearance, pleasant to the touch
- **Black nacre**: Elegant and contrasted, wear-resistant
- **White nacre**: Bright and refined, more modern style

*We can add photographic close-ups of each type during the workshop*

### 5. Decorative Grills

The grills protect the reeds while letting sound pass through. They're also a major aesthetic element!

![Collection of decorative accordion grills showing various traditional and modern patterns](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/visuels%20grille.png)

- **Over 100 models** available (geometric, floral, traditional, modern patterns)
- **Numbered**: You'll choose your grills during registration or at the start of the workshop
- **Customization possible**: We can discuss custom patterns

### 6. Bellows

The bellows are the visual soul of your accordion! It's often the most visible element.

#### Available Colors

![Accordion bellows being assembled with traditional cardboard pleats](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/Jour%205%20(7).jpg)

- **Black**: Classic and elegant
- **Red**: Vibrant and traditional
- **Blue**: Original and modern
- **Green**: Natural and fresh
- **Orange**: Joyful and bright
- **Mixed**: Combination of two colors for a unique result

![Multiple colored bellows displayed showing variety of available colors](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/Jour%205%20(26).jpg)

#### Bellows Corners
- **Standard corners**: Traditional pressed cardboard
- **Reinforced corners**: Leather corners, more resistant
- **Brushed metal corners**: Modern and robust appearance
- **Custom corners***: On request

### 7. Reeds (the voice of the accordion)

Reeds are the metal blades that vibrate to produce sound. This is a crucial element for sound quality!

- **Tipo a mano**: Standard quality reeds, excellent sound
- **A mano (+)**: Premium reeds, handcrafted, exceptional resonance (additional cost)

### 8. Accessories

#### Provided with Your Accordion
- **Straps**: Adjustable, leather or resistant fabric
- **Gig bag/carrying case**: Padded protection for transport and storage

#### Recommended for the Workshop (to bring)
- **Thin gloves**: To protect your hands during sanding
- **Safety glasses**: Provided on site but you can bring your own
- **Earplugs**: For intensive sound testing moments (optional)
- **Work clothes**: Clothes you're not afraid to get dirty (wood, glue, varnish)

## ðŸ› ï¸ The Manufacturing Process: 10 Days Step by Step

Here's a detailed overview of what you'll learn and accomplish during the 10 days of the workshop.

### Day 1: Welcome and First Assemblies

**Morning (9:30 AM-12:30 PM)**
- Welcome of trainees, introduction of each person
- Complete tour of the workshop and tools
- Presentation of safety rules
- Final choice of customization options for your accordion

**Afternoon (2:00 PM-5:30 PM)**
- Discovery of the parts prepared for your accordion
- First test assemblies
- Getting familiar with basic tools

![Group portrait of workshop participants proudly displaying their finished handcrafted accordions](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/Jour%2010%20(18)%20-%20Copie.jpg)

### Day 2: Wooden Structure

**What you'll do:**
- Assembly of the **resonance box** (right hand and left hand)
- **Sanding** surfaces for a smooth finish
- **Gluing** different parts
- **Squaring**: Checking that everything is square
- Installation of the **mechanical base**

**What you'll learn:**
- Precise woodworking techniques
- Importance of geometry for proper functioning
- Use of measuring and checking tools

![Workshop interior showing woodworking stations and tools for accordion building](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/Jour%202%20(29).jpg)

### Day 3: Keyboards and Buttons

**What you'll do:**
- Installation of **axes** that support the buttons
- Placement of **return springs**
- Mounting of keyboard **buttons**
- Adjustment of **touch feel** (resistance and travel of buttons)

**What you'll learn:**
- Fine mechanics and precision
- Importance of playing comfort
- Personalized adjustment according to your hand

![Close-up of accordion mechanics assembly, showing levers, springs and button mechanisms](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/FB%20jour%203.jpg)

### Day 4: Right Hand and Left Hand Mechanics

**What you'll do:**
- Complete assembly of **lever systems**
- Connection between buttons and valves
- Adjustment of **heights** of each button
- Adjustment of **travel** (depth of pressing)

**What you'll learn:**
- How the accordion mechanical system works
- Millimetric precision required
- Progressive testing and adjustments

![Detailed view of button alignment and keyboard setup with precision tools](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/FB%20jour%203%20bis.jpg)

### Day 5: Bellows

**What you'll do:**
- **Manufacturing** bellows with special cardboard
- Folding and gluing sections
- **Installing** bellows on the boxes
- **Adjustments** for smooth movement
- **Airtightness** tests (crucial!)

**What you'll learn:**
- Traditional bellows manufacturing technique
- Importance of airtightness for sound
- Patience and meticulousness in folding

![Accordion bellows being glued and assembled, showing cardboard pleats and pressing technique](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/Collage_20220425_180111.jpg)

### Day 6: Grills and Wood Finishing

**What you'll do:**
- **Mounting** decorative grills
- Secure fastening while maintaining aesthetics
- **Wood finishing**: Final sanding, treatment
- Application of **wax or varnish** according to your choice
- **Decoration**: Adding any personalized details

**What you'll learn:**
- Wood finishing techniques
- Protection and enhancement of wood
- Final aesthetic touches

![Decorative grill being mounted on accordion with careful positioning](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/Jour%206%20(8).jpg)

### Day 7: Reeds and First Tuning

**What you'll do:**
- **Sorting** reeds by tonality
- **Installing** reeds on reed blocks
- **First tensioning** of reeds
- **Note-by-note tests**: Checking each sound

**What you'll learn:**
- Understanding how a reed produces sound
- Recognition of tonalities
- First acoustic adjustments

![Reed installation and tuning process, showing precision work on accordion reeds](https://github.com/labodezao/stages-lutherie/blob/main/comm/Jour%207%20(23).jpg)

### Day 8: Fine Adjustments

**What you'll do:**
- Hunting for **air leaks** and correction
- Final adjustments of the **mechanics**
- Precise **alignment** of all keys
- **Responsiveness** and **comfort** tests

**What you'll learn:**
- Method for detecting problems
- Resolving imperfections
- Perfecting the operation

### Day 9: Complete Tests and Personalized Adjustments

**What you'll do:**
- **Complete tests** Right Hand + Left Hand together
- Evaluation of **playing comfort** (weight, balance, touch)
- **Personalized corrections** according to your specific needs
- Final tuning adjustments

**What you'll learn:**
- Overall evaluation of an instrument
- Adapting the instrument to your morphology
- Fine acoustic tuning

### Day 10: Musical Practice and Finalization

**Morning (9:30 AM-12:30 PM)**
- **Musical practice** with an external guest (professional musician)
- Learning **first notes** and simple melodies
- Discovery of the **musical operation** of your accordion
- **Care and maintenance** tips for your instrument

**Afternoon (2:00 PM-5:30 PM)**
- **Final tests** of each instrument
- **Photo session**: Portraits with your accordion
- **Group wrap-up**: Sharing experiences, feedback
- Handover of documents (workshop certificate, maintenance advice)
- **Celebration** and friendly moment!

## ðŸŽµ After the Workshop: Maintaining Your Accordion

### Regular Maintenance

- **Storage**: In a dry place, at stable temperature (avoid damp basement or attic)
- **Case**: Always store in the provided case when not in use
- **Cleaning**: Dust regularly with a soft cloth
- **Bellows**: Never force, open and close gently

### Use

- **Play regularly**: An accordion loves to be played!
- **Transport**: Always in its case, avoid shocks
- **Temperature**: Avoid sudden changes (hot car â†’ cold room)

### Professional Maintenance

Every **2-3 years**, have your accordion checked by a luthier:
- Tuning check
- Reed condition
- Bellows airtightness
- Mechanics operation

*Ewen offers maintenance and repair services. Contact him if needed!*
